Qullamägi’s Mean Reversion Playbook (No Fluff)
Qullamägi really only has one true “mean reversion” setup: the Parabolic Short (and its mirror, the Parabolic Long bounce). Everything revolves around the 10, 20, 50‑day MAs and VWAP.​​
1. What “Parabolic” Means
Think rubber band: move goes way too far, too fast, then snaps back to the 10/20‑day MAs.
Large caps: up ~50–100%+ in a short window; small caps: 300–1000%+.
Multiple up days, big ranges, blow‑off candles, euphoric tape.​​
2. His Mean: 10 / 20 / 50 + VWAP
Daily structure = 10, 20, 50‑day simple MAs. That’s his “map.”​​
Fast leaders: 10‑day is the key line; slower names: 20‑day.​​
VWAP intraday = timing tool for entries/exits on parabolic shorts.​​
3. Parabolic Short – Checklist
Pre‑conditions
Huge extension from the 10/20‑day, multi‑day run, massive volume.
Not day 1. He wants the late‑stage, exhausted move, not the initial squeeze.​​
Entry triggers
Break of opening‑range low (1–5 min) after a euphoric open.
First clean red 5‑min candle after a vertical open.​​
VWAP fade: first hard crack, then bounce into VWAP that fails = A+ entry.
Stops
Main stop: high of day on the reversal day.
If you use VWAP fade: stop on decisive VWAP reclaim.
Targets
Cover into the zone of the rising 10‑ and 20‑day MAs = that’s the “mean.”
Expect 1–3R singles with a very high win‑rate setup, not moonshot R multiples.
4. Parabolic Long / Washout Bounce
After a successful parabolic short, if price dumps 50–60%+ in a few days, he may flip long.​
Entries: ORH break, first strong green 5‑min bar, or intraday range break up.​
Target idea: 50–100% bounce potential as price snaps back toward prior MAs/structure.​
5. Dip Buy vs Falling Knife (Using MAs)
Buyable dip (in his world)
10 > 20 and both rising; price above a rising 50‑day = healthy uptrend.
Pullback into rising 10/20/50 with tight action or undercut‑and‑reclaim of a key MA.​​
For trend trades he sells on the first close below the 10‑day (fast) or 20‑day (slow).
Falling knife
10‑day below 20‑day and both sloping down: “probably don’t buy.”​
Price living under the 50‑day with rolling‑over MAs = downtrend, not a dip.
Wild, sloppy action at lows with no clean reclaim of VWAP or MAs.​​
6. Risk Rules You Can Steal
Per‑trade risk: ~0.25–1% of equity.​
Position size: usually 10–20% of account per name, never ~all‑in.
For big movers: take 1/3–1/2 off after 3–5 days, move stop to breakeven, trail rest with 10‑ or 20‑day.​​
Bottom line:
Short the rubber‑band stretch back to the 10/20‑day, not random weakness. 
Buy dips only when price is pulling back into rising MAs in proven leaders — everything else is a knife.​​
